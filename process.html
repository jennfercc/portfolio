My Process

My UX research experience ranges from solving problems for large enterprises to helping launch budding startups. In an ideal project, I like to conduct a range of methods to help cover both the ‘what’ and the ‘why’ of what we want to learn. Here is a taste of my typical research process & methods.

Discovery

I like to be involved in the earliest phase of a project so that I can provide key insights to help the team make informed decisions and guide product strategy. I investigate the market landscape, assess the current product, and talk to users about their experiences.

  • Heuristic evaluation
  • Competitive audit
  • Contextual inquiry
  • 1:1 user interviews
  • Stakeholder interviews
  • Contextual inquiry
  • Market surveys
  • Card Sorting

Define

Using data I collect through interviews or surveys, I then begin to define the user and map out any current processes or systems along with existing pain points. This allows the team to identify opportunities for optimization and informs ideation.

  • User personas
  • Task analysis
  • User stories
  • Mental model
  • Journey map

Test

Once the first design iteration is done, I work with the designers to identify the design hypotheses and conduct testing to validate them. Then, I share the data and user feedback with the team and make recommendations on how to fine-tune the design to best satisfy user needs.

  • Guerrilla testing
  • Usability testing
  • AB testing
